DesignSocial: Mid-Fi Prototype

DesignSocial


View Interactive Prototype

I completed this piece for a User-Centered Design course. It was an individual assignment created to demonstrate my wireframing and prototyping skills ahead of a bigger project to come (go check out Rescue-Trac if you haven't already!). Though I began the project with InVision as my tool of choice, I migrated to Figma to ensure the preservation of my work after the app's shutdown.

This illustrates a concept for an art-based social platform. Here, artists of all types can form themed communities and interact with each other online, discussing all things art. Non-artists are welcome as well, and it's a great place to commisssion artists or to find their shops that are hosted on other platforms like Etsy.

View Wireframe PDF

Screens Include:

  • a Home page displaying the communities a user is a member of. Users can join and contribute to many different themed communities on the platform
  • A Discover page with a search bar and an image grid of user posts. The posts gathered here are based on the user's interests
  • a Notifications page
  • a Profile page that includes a grid of the user's posts
  • a Messages page
  • a splash screen
  • a Settings page

Purple is chosen as the primary color because of its symbolism of creativity. Since yellow is its complementary color, I chose to use this as a secondary to contrast the cooler primary.

This layout is reminiscent of other social media interfaces to create a familiar user experience. The buttons have large touch targets for better usability and accessibility. Different font weights are used for visual interest and better visual clarity.

Geography Quiz

Geography Quiz

Visit Site

I made this in a Client-Side Scripting course as a final project. The app lets users take a simple geography quiz and optionally submit some identifying information along with it. I utilized HTML, CSS, and JavaScript in this project. Notable skills used include form validation and conditional logic. The design includes a right sidebar for ad space or other extra content.

In the making of this project, I was struggling with adding the input hints in the proper places. I also had trouble displaying the correct symbol to show whether the input was valid or needed to be fixed. In order to make it work, I placed a span tag to the right of each input box and, with JavaScript, used a switch statement to decide which hint text node it should insert into which span. The hint is offered when the input box is focused and the input is validated when the box is blurred.

Developing this app gave me a better grasp on making online quizzes, which involve conditional logic and incrementing values to total a score. It also helped me figure out how to give hints for filling in different types of input without visually overwhelming or confusing the user.

Audiophile: A Social Platform Concept

Audiophile

Marketing Website

This was from an assignment I did in a Social Platforms course. I was tasked with making a concept for a social media app, designing core pages, and then creating a site that advertised it and highlighted its key features.

Concept: This endeavor helped me learn more about how social media sites are put together. The mobile app is called "Audiophile", which would take inspiration from other platforms such as Reddit and Twitter. It would be a place for music-lovers to discuss different genres, share playlists, recommend new artists, and discuss new releases. Some of the app's features would include live-streaming, event-organizing, and Sub-Hubs, which are similar to Reddit's concept of subreddits.

View PDF

Imagery: An audiophile is someone who is enthusiastic about recording and listening to high-fidelity music. The logo I illustrated in Ibis Paint X is a stylized version of a vinyl record. This is not only a common symbol of music but also a popular listening medium among audiophiles, making it a good pick for the app's logo. The color scheme includes red because of it's symbolism of passion and excitement. These are common feelings people have when listening to and talking about their favorite music, making this color scheme really resonate with the app's users.

Interface: These screens were designed in Picsart. The top app bar includes a profile button, the app logo, and a side drawer. The bottom tab bar includes buttons for the home feed, discovery page, post creation, livestreaming, and messages. The design is high contrast, which is beneficial for accessibility.
